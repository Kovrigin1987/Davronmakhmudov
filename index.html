About

Dr. Davron Makhmudov, MD, PhD

Pediatric Infectious Disease Specialist & Quality Control Expert in Pharmaceuticals and Food Products

Biography

Education

Davron Makhmudov began his educational journey in Uzbekistan and went on to study in leading medical institutions in Russia and Europe. His academic path reflects a deep commitment to pediatrics, infectious diseases, and international standards of food and pharmaceutical quality control.

1994–2003: Davron Makhmudov attended Secondary School No. 242 in Tashkent, Uzbekistan.

2003–2006: He studied at the Academic Lyceum affiliated with the Tashkent Pediatric Medical Institute, where he earned the qualification of General Laboratory Technician.

2006: He enrolled at the Tashkent Pediatric Medical Institute to begin his medical education.

2010: He transferred to the Saint Petersburg State Pediatric Medical Academy (now the Saint Petersburg State Pediatric Medical University) in Russia.

2012: He graduated from the Saint Petersburg State Pediatric Medical Academy with an MD degree in Pediatrics.

2013–2016: He completed a Master’s program in Pediatric Infectious Diseases at the Tashkent Pediatric Medical Institute.

2016–2019: He pursued postgraduate (PhD) studies at the Saint Petersburg State Pediatric Medical University and successfully defended his candidate dissertation titled: “Modern Features of the Clinical Course of HCV Infection in Children Depending on the Virus Genotype.”

2019: His doctoral dissertation topic was officially approved in Uzbekistan: “Integration of European and American Quality Control Standards into Uzbekistan’s Pharmaceutical and Food Oversight Systems: Innovative Approaches and Global Relevance.”

2019–2020: He studied European food quality control standards at Foster Clark in Malta as part of his doctoral research.

2020–2021: He continued remote work with Foster Clark from Uzbekistan while simultaneously contributing to the national COVID-19 response at the Research Institute of Epidemiology, Microbiology, and Infectious Diseases.

2021–2024: He worked on pharmaceutical quality control at Pharmacare Premium in Malta, focusing on European compliance standards for his doctoral dissertation.

Professional Experience

After completing medical education, Dr. Davron began working across academic, clinical, and scientific sectors, later transitioning into international quality control.

2012–2016: International Department, Tashkent Pediatric Medical Institute
Organized and led the international TechReh project (EU-funded)

2012–2016: Clinical practice at the Research Institute of Epidemiology and Infectious Diseases, Tashkent

2016–2019: PhD student, lecturer, and researcher at Saint Petersburg Pediatric Medical University

2016–2019: Pediatric Infectious Disease Specialist at the Research Institute of Pediatric Infectious Diseases, St. Petersburg

2019–2021: Quality Control Analyst at Foster Clark (Malta), specializing in food safety

2020–2021: Returned to Uzbekistan to assist in COVID-19 treatment efforts as an infectious disease expert
Received the "Excellence in Healthcare" Medal

2021–2024: Quality Control Analyst at Pharmacare Premium (Malta), focusing on:

  • Post-production pharmaceutical analysis
  • Regulatory compliance with EU standards
  • Clinical relevance evaluation of drug formulations

2025: President of Uzbekistan Infectious Disease Association www.uzbekistaninfectiousdiseaseassociation.org

Professional Achievements, Honors & Awards

Dr. Davron Makhmudov is the author of two medical books and holds a PhD in Pediatric Infectious Diseases. He has made significant contributions to both academic research and public health. During the COVID-19 pandemic, he served on the front lines in Uzbekistan and was awarded the “Excellence in Healthcare” medal by the Ministry of Health for his outstanding service. His achievements include scientific publications, international collaboration, and participation in major public health initiatives. Dr. Makhmudov continues to be recognized for his efforts to improve healthcare quality and infectious disease management on a global level.
